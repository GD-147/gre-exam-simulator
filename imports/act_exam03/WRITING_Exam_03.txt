WRITING Exam 3PART A č WRITING PROMPTW3-001Prompt:As digital tools become more common in schools, some teachers are rethinking how students should take notes, organize ideas, and prepare for exams. Some educators argue that students should continue using handwritten notes because writing by hand can improve focus and memory. Others argue that digital note-taking is more practical because it allows students to search, store, and revise information more efficiently. Still others believe that the best approach is not to choose one method for everyone, but to teach students how to use each method effectively depending on the task.Issue:Should schools encourage students to rely mainly on handwritten note-taking, mainly on digital note-taking, or on a flexible combination of both?Perspective 1:Schools should encourage handwritten note-taking because it slows students down in a useful way and helps them process information more carefully.Perspective 2:Schools should encourage digital note-taking because it is more efficient, easier to organize, and better suited to the way students learn and work today.Perspective 3:Schools should teach students to use both handwritten and digital note-taking, because the most effective method depends on the subject, task, and student.Essay Task:Write a unified, coherent essay in which you:* clearly state your own perspective on the issue* analyze the relationship between your perspective and at least 1 other perspective* develop your ideas with logical reasoning and specific examples* organize your ideas clearly* use precise language and correct grammar and punctuationPART B č SCORING GUIDEScoring Focus:* Ideas and Analysis* Development and Support* Organization* Language Use and Conventions* Ideas and Analysis: A strong response presents a clear perspective on the issue, considers its complexity, and explains how that perspective relates to at least 1 other perspective.* Development and Support: A strong response develops its claims with logical reasoning and specific, relevant examples rather than general statements alone.* Organization: A strong response is clearly structured, with a purposeful introduction, connected body paragraphs, and a conclusion that reinforces the central argument.* Language Use and Conventions: A strong response uses precise word choice, varied sentence structure, and generally correct grammar, usage, and punctuation.
