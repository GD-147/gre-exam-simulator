PART A � WRITING PROMPT
W1-001
Prompt:
Schools are under increasing pressure to prepare students not only for tests and graduation requirements but also for a fast-changing future. As a result, some schools are considering setting aside regular time during the school week for student-directed projects, independent research, or creative problem-solving. Supporters argue that this kind of time helps students become more curious, adaptable, and engaged. Critics argue that schools already struggle to cover essential content and should focus on core academic instruction. Others believe that student-directed work can be valuable, but only if it is closely connected to course goals and clear standards.
Issue:
Should schools devote regular instructional time to student-directed learning, or should they focus mainly on structured academic instruction?
Perspective 1:
Schools should devote regular time to student-directed learning because students need opportunities to think independently, explore real interests, and develop skills that cannot be built through memorization alone.
Perspective 2:
Schools should focus mainly on structured academic instruction because limited class time should be used to strengthen essential knowledge and skills that all students need.
Perspective 3:
Student-directed learning can be worthwhile, but it should be included only when it is tied to clear academic goals and guided closely enough to ensure meaningful progress.
Essay Task:
Write a unified, coherent essay in which you:
* clearly state your own perspective on the issue
* analyze the relationship between your perspective and at least 1 other perspective
* develop your ideas with logical reasoning and specific examples
* organize your ideas clearly
* use precise language and correct grammar and punctuation
PART B � SCORING GUIDE
Scoring Focus:
* Ideas and Analysis
* Development and Support
* Organization
* Language Use and Conventions
* Ideas and Analysis: A strong response presents a clear, thoughtful perspective and examines the issue in a nuanced way, including how that perspective relates to at least one other perspective.
* Development and Support: A strong response explains its reasoning fully and uses relevant, specific examples to support its claims.
* Organization: A strong response is logically arranged, with a clear introduction, purposeful body paragraphs, and a conclusion that reinforces the essay�s central argument.
* Language Use and Conventions: A strong response uses precise word choice, varied sentence structure, and generally correct grammar, usage, and punctuation.
