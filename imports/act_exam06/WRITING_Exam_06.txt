PART A � WRITING PROMPT
W6-001
Prompt:
As schools try to prepare students for an increasingly connected world, many educators are debating whether learning should happen mostly within traditional subject areas or whether students should spend more time on interdisciplinary courses and projects. Some argue that schools should keep subjects clearly separate because students need strong foundations in each discipline before they can combine them effectively. Others argue that real-world problems do not fit neatly into one subject, so schools should emphasize interdisciplinary learning that connects science, history, writing, mathematics, and the arts. Still others believe that interdisciplinary learning can be valuable, but only when students first receive enough structured instruction in the core subjects that support it.
Issue:
Should schools place greater emphasis on interdisciplinary learning, or should they focus mainly on teaching subjects separately?
Perspective 1:
Schools should focus mainly on teaching subjects separately because students need deep knowledge in individual disciplines before they can apply that knowledge well.
Perspective 2:
Schools should place greater emphasis on interdisciplinary learning because students are better prepared for real life when they learn to connect ideas across different subjects.
Perspective 3:
Schools should combine both approaches, using interdisciplinary learning only when students have already built strong foundations in the core subjects involved.
Essay Task:
Write a unified, coherent essay in which you:
* clearly state your own perspective on the issue
* analyze the relationship between your perspective and at least 1 other perspective
* develop your ideas with logical reasoning and specific examples
* organize your ideas clearly
* use precise language and correct grammar and punctuation
PART B � SCORING GUIDE
Scoring Focus:
* Ideas and Analysis
* Development and Support
* Organization
* Language Use and Conventions
* Ideas and Analysis: A strong response presents a clear perspective on the issue, addresses its complexity, and explains how that perspective relates to at least 1 other perspective.
* Development and Support: A strong response supports its claims with logical reasoning and specific, relevant examples rather than broad generalizations alone.
* Organization: A strong response is clearly structured, with a focused introduction, connected body paragraphs, and a conclusion that reinforces the essay�s central argument.
* Language Use and Conventions: A strong response uses precise word choice, varied sentence structure, and generally correct grammar, usage, and punctuation.
