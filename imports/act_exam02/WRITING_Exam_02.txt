WRITING Exam 2PART A č WRITING PROMPTW2-001Prompt:In many schools, class participation has traditionally been measured by how often students speak during discussions. However, classrooms now include students with different communication styles, language backgrounds, and levels of confidence. Some educators argue that frequent speaking should remain the main standard because discussion is essential to learning and civic life. Others argue that participation should be measured more broadly, including listening, note-taking, written responses, and collaborative work. Still others believe that speaking should matter, but schools should give students multiple structured ways to participate so that expectations remain high without favoring only one style of communication.Issue:How should schools define and evaluate student participation in class?Perspective 1:Class participation should continue to be judged mainly by how often students speak, because discussion is the clearest sign that students are engaged and thinking actively.Perspective 2:Participation should be evaluated more broadly, because students can contribute meaningfully through listening, writing, preparation, and collaboration as well as through speaking.Perspective 3:Speaking in class should remain important, but schools should use a wider range of structured participation methods so that students are challenged fairly.Essay Task:Write a unified, coherent essay in which you:* clearly state your own perspective on the issue* analyze the relationship between your perspective and at least 1 other perspective* develop your ideas with logical reasoning and specific examples* organize your ideas clearly* use precise language and correct grammar and punctuationPART B č SCORING GUIDEScoring Focus:* Ideas and Analysis* Development and Support* Organization* Language Use and Conventions* Ideas and Analysis: A strong response presents a clear perspective, considers the complexity of the issue, and explains how that perspective relates to at least 1 other perspective.* Development and Support: A strong response uses relevant reasoning and specific examples to explain and support its claims.* Organization: A strong response is logically structured, with a clear progression of ideas and effective transitions between points.* Language Use and Conventions: A strong response uses precise language, varied sentence structure, and generally correct grammar, usage, and punctuation.
