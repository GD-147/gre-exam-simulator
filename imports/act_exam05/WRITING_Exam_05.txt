WRITING Exam 5
PART A Ð WRITING PROMPT
W5-001
Prompt:
As artificial intelligence tools become easier for students to access, schools are deciding how these tools should fit into learning. Some educators argue that students should be allowed to use AI tools regularly because these tools reflect the technology students will encounter in college and future careers. Others argue that schools should sharply limit AI use because too much reliance on it may weaken students€ ability to think independently and communicate clearly. Still others believe AI can be useful in education, but only when it is used in carefully defined ways that support learning rather than replace it.
Issue:
How should schools respond to student use of artificial intelligence tools in academic work?
Perspective 1:
Schools should allow students to use AI tools regularly because learning to use powerful new technology responsibly is now an important part of education.
Perspective 2:
Schools should sharply limit student use of AI tools because students need to develop their own reasoning, writing, and problem-solving skills without depending on technology to do that work for them.
Perspective 3:
Schools should allow AI tools only in limited, clearly defined ways so that students can benefit from the technology without letting it replace genuine learning.
Essay Task:
Write a unified, coherent essay in which you:
* clearly state your own perspective on the issue
* analyze the relationship between your perspective and at least 1 other perspective
* develop your ideas with logical reasoning and specific examples
* organize your ideas clearly
* use precise language and correct grammar and punctuation
PART B Ð SCORING GUIDE
Scoring Focus:
* Ideas and Analysis
* Development and Support
* Organization
* Language Use and Conventions
* Ideas and Analysis: A strong response presents a clear perspective on the issue, considers its complexity, and explains how that perspective relates to at least 1 other perspective.
* Development and Support: A strong response develops its argument with relevant reasoning and specific examples rather than relying only on broad generalizations.
* Organization: A strong response is logically structured, with a focused introduction, connected body paragraphs, and a conclusion that reinforces the central argument.
* Language Use and Conventions: A strong response uses precise word choice, varied sentence structure, and generally correct grammar, usage, and punctuation.
